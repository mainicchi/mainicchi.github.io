Painovoimaa

New graphic identity, focusing on accessibility and from that an updated website design

Painovoimaa is an initiative project at Metropolia University of Applied Sciences that aims to revamp printing technology studies at the higher education level. The initiative project emphasizes the importance of traditional printing methods alongside digital advancements.

This project consisted of creating a new graphic identity, focusing on accessibility and engaging visuals and of that to create an updated website. Throughout the 25-week project, collaboration, UI design, and iterative layout processes were central. The results were a visually compelling and accessible website.

This project highlights my expertise in transforming visual identity and delivering a modern, user-friendly website tailored to client needs.

The long story

The existing Painovoimaa Initiative website contained outdated information, with maintenance complexities arising from previous developers' choices of WordPress plugins. The aim was a comprehensive external makeover and enhanced search engine optimization targeting both students and businesses.

So the project involved creating a new graphic identity and guidelines for the website. The client also whished for a visually engaging representation of the field, emphasizing accessibility on the website.

The old website had outdated infomation and didn't fit the clients vision for the brand.

Process

Spanning 25 weeks from August to December, the project involved content restructuring, with the team collaborating closely through live meetings and Teams sessions. Client consultations, workshops, and vision development were integral parts of the process.

The UI design process included sketching, benchmarking other project websites, user testing, and creating preliminary wireframes. Testing and feedback guided the refinement of wireframes, incorporating the new graphic identity. Multiple layout versions were presented to the client, who chose preferred features for the construction of the final version. The chosen elements were chosen into a cohesive, final version. For ongoing website management, two sets of written instructions and a video guide for adding articles were provided to the client.

The design process included sketching, using personas, benchmarking and user testing.

As a UX designer, I created wireframes in this project, among other tasks.

Results

The chosen style for the Painovoimaa Initiative is clear, illustrative, and refreshing, aligning with Metropolia's visual identity while maintaining a distinctive character. Crucially, the selected style prioritizes accessibility, ensuring ease of guidance and implementation. For ongoing website management, two sets of written instructions and a video guide for adding articles were provided to the client.

This project exemplifies my ability to deliver a visually compelling website, incorporating client preferences, and overcoming challenges for a successful outcome. This project also serves as a testament to my proficiency in transforming a brand's visual identity, overcoming challenges, and delivering a modern, user-friendly website tailored to the specific needs of our client.
