Metropolia intranet

Researching and making a better intranet with service design methods and transcribing the end product to a cohesive pamphlet for the client.

I took part in Metropolia's intranet reform project. Our team was responsible of the research for operational fluidity of the site. And I was also part of a two person team that transcribed the end product to a cohesive pamphlet for the client.

The project included fundamental skills of service design in practical use, for example user research and testing with various design methods. In user research we had intranet users help us find the pros and cons by using the card sorting method and open interviews.

The long story

The client asked us to find out what kind of content and functionalities make studying smoother. The client wanted to also know what kind of interaction needs the intranet could fulfill and what is needed to make it attractive to users who go to the intranet.

In the assignment, user profiles, for example, were raised as an interesting research subjects. Who are the users? How and what is the OMA intranet used for? How does it feel to use? The goal was to create a concrete vision of the OMA intranet and find an answer to the question: what is a dialogic intranet like, that supports the performance of its basic tasks and enables interaction?

Process

We started the project by analysing the most important questions of the assignment, as well as the prioritized wishes of the users and the current challenges of the intranet. Based on the research, the project was separated into two different main themes; the intranet's dialogic interaction and the intranet's basic task functionalities to help everyday work.

The functional team focused on the information architecture of the intranet, its functionality, and its content. The dialogic team covered the interaction with users and the community and intranet visual elements. Our team worked on this theme. Also me and and other teammate consolidated information from all the teams and translated the end product into a cohesive pamphlet for the client.

Our team facilitated various workshops and open interviews with the university's students, teachers, and other workforces, who used OMA intranet regularly.

Results

From the workshops and interviews, we found a lot of different opinions and clear improvement possibilities. The challenge was to find the golden middle ground of the different users needs and cases. There were a lot of differences between the student's and other workforce's intranet needs.
